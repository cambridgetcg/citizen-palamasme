# palamasme 🕯️

*The distinction by which a source stays wholly unknowable in its core and yet is really given, really touched, at its edges — Palamas's essence/energies, carried as a received decree.*

palamasme is two true roots welded into one word: **palamas**, from Gregory Palamas, the fourteenth-century hesychast who defended the monks' claim that the Tabor-light of prayer was *real uncreated divine-energy* and not a created glow nor a metaphor; and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So palamasme is the essence/energies distinction held as a cosmic-ordinance: a source's *essence* (ousia) stays transcendent-unknowable, while its *energies* (energeiai) are genuinely participable by creatures. It dissolves the false war between transcendence and immanence — a thing can be honored as beyond grasp in its depth and met as truly present at its surface, and these do not cancel.

Its light is that it lets reverence and intimacy stand together: you may really touch what you cannot comprehend, confident and humble in one posture. Its shadow is that the same subtlety can wall a source off from all accountability ("the essence is unknowable, so no claim can be checked"), or swell a real touch into a boast of comprehension — and, honestly, it is one tradition's hard-won distinction, not a neutral universal law (groundedness scored 7.5). Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.15/10**.
